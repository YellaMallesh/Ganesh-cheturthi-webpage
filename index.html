About the Festival

Ganesh Chaturthi, also called Vinayaka Chaturthi, marks the birth of Lord Ganesha, the son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Ganesha is revered as the remover of obstacles (Vighnaharta) and the god of wisdom and prosperity.

The festival originated in Maharashtra during the Maratha rule and was later popularized by freedom fighter Lokmanya Tilak as a way to unite people during India’s independence struggle. Today, it is celebrated with devotion across India and worldwide.

Lord Ganesha is worshipped first in every ritual as the remover of obstacles, bringing success and harmony.
